About

After working in retail for over 16 years, selling high end leather goods in Aspinal of London and Harrods or the latest gaming tech in GAME, I have started a new career in web design and development with a focus on accessibility and the user experience. As different as these two careers may seem at first glance I have found there are quite a few similarities between them, and a lot of my experiences are transferable and beneficial. The customer service of in person sales is the user experience of web design and development.

Much of what I enjoyed most about working in retail was helping people find what it was they wanted and making the whole experience a positive one, and that is exactly how I feel about being a front-end developer. From creating an eye-catching window display and reorganising display units, to building a rapport for customers and staying up to date with pertinent information, all of this is encapsulated in the role of a web designer/developer. I take pride in my attention to customer service, branding and accessibility, and look forward to applying my experience in customer service to new opportunities in web UX/UI. In the end: new name, same game.

Speaking of games, my greatest and longest lasting passion is video games. From a young age they were my favourite medium of creativity and storytelling, as well as challenge and escapism. More recently I have considered trying to get into the video games industry, and the idea of designing and developing websites for games, developers or publishers is a very exciting prospect to me! Building a unique space for fans to visit and congregate to share their excitement and receive updates, a place brimming with personality, all put together for an enjoyable experience.
